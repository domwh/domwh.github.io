2. Goodfellas

Coming in at number 2 is a movie in everyone's top ten, Goodfellas. The Martin Scorsese film stars Ray Liotta as Henry Hill, a young gangster who is living the dream. Henry befriends fellow gangsters Tommy DeVito and Jimmy Conway, and the three get into some serious trouble and serious cash. Henry's live style proves to be a little too much for his wife, Karen, and she becomes increasingly frustrated with him. This movie just doesn't get old, and the chaotic life of Henry Hill somehow makes you wish you were and weren't a gangster at the same time.

1. The Wolf of Wall Street

My favorite movie is Martin Scorsese's over the top, in your face, laugh out loud masterpiece The Wolf of Wall Street. The film follows the life of Jordan Belfort, played by Leonardo DiCaprio, who is a successful stockbroker and salesman who later starts his own brokerage firm. Jordan makes millions and parties hard with his friends, but not all of his firms practices are legal. With Jordan's greed and drug addiction increasing more and more throughout the movie, reality is destined to catch up with him. This movie has a great plot, great production, and great acting from DiCaprio, Jonah Hill, and Margot Robbie. With all that combined it puts it at the top of the list of my favorite movies.
